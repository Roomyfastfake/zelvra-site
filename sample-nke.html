NKE · NIKE, Inc.

The $0.72 asterisk: a quadrupled EPS that is mostly a court ruling

Source-verified
Source packet NIKE Form 8-K Exhibit 99.1, June 30, 2026 (sec.gov)
Consensus source CNBC compiled estimates, June 30, 2026

Hook

Nike's headline Q4 EPS of $0.72 is up 407% — but $0.52 of it is a one-time tariff refund the Supreme Court handed back in February. Strip it out and this is a $0.20 quarter with revenue still shrinking. The real story is where the shrinking has stopped, and where it hasn't.

The 5 numbers

  • Revenue$10.97B, −1% reported / −4% currency-neutral [ER: headline bullets] · consensus ~$10.86B [CNBC]
  • Diluted EPS$0.72 including a $0.52 IEEPA tariff-recovery benefit → ~$0.20 ex-item (derived) vs $0.14 last year [ER: headline bullets; income statement] · consensus ~$0.13 [CNBC]
  • Gross margin49.2%, +890bps — but ~900bps came from the $986M expected tariff recovery; underlying margin roughly flat (derived) [ER: Q4 Income Statement Review]
  • The channel reversalWholesale +4% ($6.6B) vs NIKE Direct −7% ($4.1B), Digital −12% — the DTC-first era is formally running in reverse [ER: Q4 Income Statement Review]
  • Buried detailOnly ~$0.3B of the $986M recovery arrived as cash; the rest is a receivable (AR +26% YoY to $5.9B). The EPS quadrupling is, for now, mostly non-cash [ER: Balance Sheet Review; balance sheet]

Beat / miss vs consensus

Beat on both lines against reduced expectations: adjusted EPS ~$0.20 vs ~$0.13 expected; revenue $10.97B vs ~$10.86B expected [CNBC, Jun 30, 2026 — third-party compiled].

Bull lens

North America is growing again (Q4 +3%, footwear +4%; full-year +5%) and the wholesale reset is producing growth with a clean channel — inventories flat YoY at $7.5B [ER: Divisional Revenues; Balance Sheet Review]. A real cash tailwind is queued: ~$686M of the tariff recovery still to be collected (derived), on top of S&A down 2% and a dividend up 5% [ER: Q4 review; Shareholder Returns].

Bear lens

Greater China is getting worse (Q4 −12% reported / −17% currency-neutral; full-year China EBIT −20%) and the high-margin channels are still the ones shrinking: Digital −12%, ex-tariff gross margin flat [ER: Divisional Revenues; EBIT table; Q4 review]. Brand heat problems extend beyond the swoosh — Converse −32% in Q4, full-year Converse EBIT down 93%; Jordan Brand −3% [ER: Divisional Revenues + footnote 3; EBIT table].
