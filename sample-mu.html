MU · Micron Technology

The biggest quarter in memory history is almost pure price

Source-verified
Source packet Micron press release, June 24, 2026 (investors.micron.com)
Consensus source Investing.com compiled estimates, June 25, 2026

Hook

Micron just reported the largest quarter in memory-industry history — $41.5B of revenue, up ~346% in a year — and guided the next quarter $8.5B higher. The number nobody is leading with: cost of goods barely moved. This quarter is almost pure price.

The 5 numbers

  • Revenue$41.46B vs $23.86B prior qtr, $9.30B a year ago — +346% YoY, +74% QoQ (derived) [PR: FQ3-26 highlights] · consensus ~$35.69B [Investing.com]
  • Non-GAAP diluted EPS$25.11 vs $1.91 a year ago [PR: Quarterly Financial Results] · consensus ~$20.49 [Investing.com]
  • Guidance (FQ4-26)Revenue $50.0B ± $1.0B · gross margin ~86% · non-GAAP EPS $31.00 ± $1.00 — implies +21% QoQ (derived) [PR: Business Outlook]
  • The surpriseGAAP gross margin 84.6% vs 37.7% a year ago [PR: Quarterly Financial Results]
  • Buried detailCOGS $6.40B, up only ~10% YoY while revenue rose ~346% — the explosion is price/mix on constrained supply, not units [PR: Consolidated Statements of Operations; % derived]

Beat / miss vs consensus

Beat compiled consensus on revenue by ~16% and non-GAAP EPS by ~23%; shares rose ~14.6% following the report [Investing.com, Jun 25, 2026 — third-party compiled].

Bull lens

Visibility is being contractualized: multi-year "Strategic Customer Agreements" the CEO says will "significantly enhance the durability and predictability" of results, HBM4 already in high-volume shipment, HBM4E slated for calendar 2027 [PR: CEO quote; Product highlights]. Guided margin (~86%) is above the record just printed. Long-term debt cut from $14.0B to $5.1B since August; $30.2B cash and investments; $18.3B adjusted free cash flow this quarter [PR: balance sheet; highlights].

Bear lens

A price cycle, not a volume cycle — and memory pricing mean-reverts. Receivables have grown to $31.0B from $9.3B last August; growth in receivables consumed $20.0B of cash over nine months [PR: balance sheet; cash flow statement]. Capex nearly doubled (nine-month PP&E spend $19.6B vs $10.2B) [PR: cash flow statement] — record supply investment at cycle highs is the classic setup for the next downturn.
