## HOOK

Micron just reported the largest quarter in memory-industry history — $41.5B of revenue,
up ~346% in a year — and guided the next quarter $8.5B higher. The number nobody is
leading with: cost of goods barely moved. This quarter is almost pure price.

## THE 5 NUMBERS

1. **Revenue: $41.46B** — vs $23.86B prior quarter and $9.30B a year ago
   [PR: Fiscal Q3 2026 highlights] → **+346% YoY, +74% QoQ** (derived). Consensus was
   ~$35.69B [Investing.com, Jun 25, 2026].
2. **Non-GAAP diluted EPS: $25.11** — vs $12.20 prior quarter, $1.91 a year ago
   [PR: Quarterly Financial Results table]. Consensus was ~$20.49 [Investing.com, Jun 25, 2026].
3. **Guidance: FQ4-26 revenue $50.0B ± $1.0B, non-GAAP EPS $31.00 ± $1.00, gross margin ~86%**
   [PR: Business Outlook table] → implies another **+21% QoQ** at the midpoint (derived).
4. **The surprise: GAAP gross margin 84.6%** — vs 74.4% prior quarter and 37.7% a year ago
   [PR: Quarterly Financial Results table]. For a commodity-memory maker this is an
   unprecedented margin structure.
5. **Buried detail: cost of goods sold was $6.40B — up only ~10% YoY** (vs $5.79B in FQ3-25)
   while revenue rose ~346% [PR: Consolidated Statements of Operations; % derived].
   Units and cost base barely grew; the entire explosion is price/mix on constrained
   supply. That cuts both ways (see bear case).

## BEAT / MISS vs CONSENSUS

Beat compiled consensus on revenue by ~16% ($41.46B vs ~$35.69B) and on non-GAAP EPS by
~23% ($25.11 vs ~$20.49); shares rose ~14.6% following the report
[Investing.com, Jun 25, 2026 — third-party compiled figures, not company-provided].

## BULL CASE

- **Visibility is being contractualized.** CEO Sanjay Mehrotra: multi-year "Strategic
  Customer Agreements" are expected to "significantly enhance the durability and
  predictability" of results [PR: CEO quote]. HBM4 is already in high-volume shipment
  for the lead customer's platform, with HBM4E volume production expected calendar 2027
  [PR: Product highlights].
- **Margins are still rising.** Guided gross margin of ~86% for FQ4 is above the record
  84.6% just printed [PR: Business Outlook table].
- **The balance sheet transformed in nine months.** Long-term debt cut from $14.0B
  (Aug 2025) to $5.1B; cash + investments of $30.2B; adjusted free cash flow of $18.3B
  this quarter alone [PR: Consolidated Balance Sheets; highlights].

## BEAR CASE

- **A price cycle, not a volume cycle.** COGS +10% YoY vs revenue +346% [PR: income
  statement; derived] means today's economics rest on memory pricing that history says
  mean-reverts. Any supply catch-up compresses margin from an extreme starting point.
- **Cash lags the P&L.** Receivables have grown to $31.0B from $9.3B last August
  [PR: Consolidated Balance Sheets] — growth in receivables consumed $20.0B of cash over
  nine months [PR: Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows]. Revenue is being booked much
  faster than it is collected.
- **Capex is doubling into the boom.** Nine-month property/plant/equipment spend of
  $19.6B vs $10.2B a year earlier [PR: cash flow statement]. Record supply investment at
  cycle highs is the classic memory-industry setup for oversupply later.
