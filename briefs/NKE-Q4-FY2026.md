## HOOK

Nike's headline Q4 EPS of $0.72 is up 407% — but $0.52 of it is a one-time tariff refund
the Supreme Court handed back in February. Strip it out and this is a $0.20 quarter with
revenue still shrinking. The real story is *where* the shrinking has stopped, and where
it hasn't.

## THE 5 NUMBERS

1. **Revenue: $10.97B, down 1% reported / down 4% currency-neutral** [ER: headline
   bullets]. Consensus was ~$10.86B [CNBC, Jun 30, 2026] — a slim beat of a low bar.
2. **Diluted EPS: $0.72, including a $0.52 benefit from expected IEEPA tariff recovery**
   [ER: headline bullets] → **~$0.20 ex-item** (derived), vs $0.14 a year ago
   [ER: income statement] and ~$0.13 expected [CNBC, Jun 30, 2026].
3. **Gross margin: 49.2%, +890bps YoY — but ~900bps of that is the $986M expected
   tariff recovery** [ER: Q4 Income Statement Review]. Underlying gross margin was
   roughly flat vs last year's 40.3% (derived).
4. **The channel reversal: Wholesale +4% ($6.6B) vs NIKE Direct −7% ($4.1B), with
   NIKE Brand Digital −12%** [ER: Q4 Income Statement Review]. The decade-long
   DTC-first strategy is now formally running in reverse.
5. **Buried detail: only ~$0.3B of the $986M tariff recovery arrived as cash this year**
   [ER: Balance Sheet Review] — the rest is an expected receivable (accounts receivable
   +26% YoY to $5.9B [ER: balance sheet]). The EPS quadrupling is, for now, mostly
   non-cash. The Supreme Court ruled IEEPA tariffs unauthorized on Feb 20, 2026; Nike
   deemed recovery "probable" this quarter [ER: EBIT table, footnote 2].

## BEAT / MISS vs CONSENSUS

Beat on both lines, against reduced expectations: adjusted EPS ~$0.20 vs ~$0.13
expected; revenue $10.97B vs ~$10.86B expected [CNBC, Jun 30, 2026 — third-party
compiled figures, not company-provided].

## BULL CASE

- **North America is growing again.** Q4 revenue +3% (footwear +4%); full-year +5%
  [ER: Divisional Revenues]. The largest segment has reaccelerated while China and
  EMEA get fixed.
- **The wholesale reset is working and the channel is clean.** Wholesale +4% in Q4 and
  +6% for the year; inventories flat YoY at $7.5B [ER: Divisional Revenues; Balance
  Sheet Review] — growth without channel stuffing.
- **A real cash tailwind is queued.** ~$686M of the $986M tariff recovery is still to be
  collected (derived from [ER: Balance Sheet Review]), on top of S&A discipline
  (−2% in Q4) and a dividend up 5% for the year [ER: Q4 review; Shareholder Returns].

## BEAR CASE

- **Greater China is getting worse, not better.** Q4 revenue −12% reported / −17%
  currency-neutral; full-year China EBIT −20% [ER: Divisional Revenues; EBIT table].
- **The high-margin channel is still the one shrinking.** Digital −12% in Q4 and −12%
  for the year [ER: Q4 and FY reviews]; ex-tariff gross margin flat means no underlying
  margin repair yet (derived from [ER: Q4 review]).
- **Brand heat problems beyond the swoosh.** Converse −32% in Q4 to $244M, full-year
  Converse EBIT down 93% to $18M; Jordan Brand −3% for the year to $7.03B
  [ER: Divisional Revenues + footnote 3; EBIT table].

## READY-TO-PUBLISH ASSET — newsletter section (~150 words)

**Nike's $0.72 asterisk.** Nike closed fiscal 2026 with headline Q4 EPS up 407% to
$0.72 — and almost none of it came from selling shoes. $0.52 per share is the expected
refund of IEEPA tariffs the Supreme Court struck down in February; underlying EPS was
about $0.20 on revenue of $11.0B, down 1%. The cleaner signal sits in the segments.
North America grew 3% and wholesale rose 4% — the strategy U-turn back toward retail
partners is producing growth with inventories flat at $7.5B. But the channels Nike
spent a decade building are still contracting: digital fell 12%, Greater China dropped
12% (17% ex-currency), and Converse collapsed 32%. One more thing: only ~$0.3B of that
$986M refund is cash so far — the rest is a receivable. Fiscal 2027 gets the cash;
fiscal 2026 got the optics. *Not investment advice.*
