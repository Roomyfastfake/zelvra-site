Netflix (NFLX) — Q2 2026 Earnings Brief

Quarter ended June 30, 2026 · Reported July 16, 2026 · Prepared by Zelvra

Creator Hooks

Hook A — The guidance story (stock -9%)

Netflix beat Q2. Shares fell 9%. That gap tells you everything about where the market's attention actually is — and it's not on last quarter's revenue.

Hook B — The subscriber number they stopped reporting

Netflix has 325 million subscribers. They stopped reporting how many they added this quarter. When a company stops disclosing a number it used to lead every earnings call with, that's worth a paragraph in your next issue.

Hook C — The ad tier angle

Netflix's ad-supported tier now reaches 250 million monthly active viewers. Ad revenue is on track for $3 billion this year — double last year. The company that spent a decade refusing ads just built one of the largest ad platforms on the internet.

Hook D — Revenue deceleration framing

Netflix grew revenue 16% in Q1. It grew 13% in Q2. It's guiding to 12% in Q3. The direction is clear. The question for creators covering this story: is this a new normal, or the floor before re-acceleration?

One-Paragraph Summary

Netflix reported Q2 2026 revenue of $12.56 billion, up 13.4% year over year, marginally below the $12.59 billion Wall Street consensus. Diluted EPS of $0.80 edged past the $0.79 estimate and grew 11% from $0.72 in Q2 2025. Net income came in at $3.40 billion. Operating income was $4.2 billion, up 11% year over year, but operating margin compressed slightly to 33.4% from 34.1% a year ago. The company no longer reports paid net subscriber additions — global membership stands at 325 million. The ad-supported tier reached 250 million monthly active viewers worldwide, and full-year ad revenue remains on track for approximately $3 billion, roughly double 2025 levels. For Q3, Netflix guided to 12% revenue growth and a 33.2% operating margin — the latter actually a significant improvement from 28.2% in Q3 2025. Shares fell approximately 9% after hours, hitting a 52-week low, as the market focused on the slight revenue miss and decelerating top-line growth trajectory rather than the margin improvement embedded in guidance.

The 5 Numbers That Matter

1. Revenue — $12.56 billion

  • YoY change: +13.4% (from $11.08B in Q2 2025)
  • vs. consensus: $12.56B vs. $12.59B estimate (miss of ~$30M, ~0.25%)
  • Context: Growth has decelerated from 16.2% in Q1 2026 to 13.4% in Q2. Q3 guidance implies further deceleration to ~12%. EMEA crossed $4.0B quarterly for the first time. LATAM and APAC each crossed $1.5B. All regions grew double digits.
  • Source: Netflix Q2 2026 Earnings Press Release / Shareholder Letter, ir.netflix.net; CNBC Q2 2026 earnings coverage

2. EPS — $0.80 diluted (non-GAAP basis comparable)

  • YoY change: +11.1% (from $0.72 in Q2 2025)
  • vs. consensus: $0.80 vs. $0.79 estimate (beat)
  • Net income: $3.40 billion vs. $3.13 billion in Q2 2025 (+8.6% YoY)
  • Note: No one-time items of the scale seen in Q1 2026 ($2.8B Warner Bros. termination fee). This is a cleaner quarter operationally.
  • Source: Netflix Q2 2026 Earnings Press Release, ir.netflix.net; Investing.com earnings call transcript summary

3. Operating Margin — 33.4%

  • YoY change: -70 bps (from 34.1% in Q2 2025)
  • Operating income: $4.2 billion (+11% YoY)
  • Q3 guide: 33.2% operating margin — a 500 bps improvement vs. Q3 2025's 28.2%
  • Context: Slight compression in Q2, but the Q3 margin guide is the more important number. A 5-point YoY margin improvement in Q3 would represent significant operating leverage kicking in even as revenue growth slows.
  • Source: Netflix Q2 2026 Shareholder Letter, ir.netflix.net; The Wrap Q2 2026 earnings coverage

4. Ad Tier — 250 million monthly active viewers

  • Context: Disclosed at Netflix's May 2026 Upfront presentation. Netflix does not break out ad revenue separately by quarter but confirmed full-year 2026 ad revenue remains on track for ~$3 billion, approximately 2x 2025 levels.
  • Why it matters: 250 million MAUs on an ad-supported tier is a larger advertising reach than most traditional TV networks. For finance creators covering the streaming or digital advertising space, this is the structural story inside the earnings report.
  • Source: Netflix May 2026 Upfront presentation; Deadline Q2 2026 earnings coverage

5. Q3 2026 Revenue Guidance — 12% growth (~$13.0B implied)

  • Full-year guidance (narrowed): $51.0B–$51.4B (from $50.7B–$51.7B previously)
  • Context: Netflix narrowed its full-year range while keeping the midpoint roughly flat. The Q3 guide implies another sequential deceleration from Q2's 13.4%. This — not the Q2 number — is what sent the stock down 9% after hours. Markets were pricing in either re-acceleration or a more constructive guide.
  • Source: Netflix Q2 2026 Shareholder Letter, ir.netflix.net; Variety Q2 2026 earnings coverage

Bull Case vs. Bear Case

Bull Case

  • Q3 operating margin guide of 33.2% is 500 bps above Q3 2025 — profit growing faster than revenue is the definition of operating leverage
  • Ad business reaching $3B in 2026 (2x YoY) with 250M MAUs creates a second revenue engine that was nonexistent three years ago
  • Double-digit growth in all four geographic segments — this is not a one-region story
  • Full-year guidance narrowed, not lowered — the range is tighter, not worse
  • Content amortization growth expected to peak in Q2 and decelerate in H2 — cost structure improving into year-end

Bear Case

  • Revenue growth trajectory: 16% (Q1) → 13% (Q2) → 12% guided (Q3) — three consecutive quarters of deceleration
  • Stopped reporting net paid subscriber additions — the metric that drove the stock for a decade is gone; market has less visibility into demand
  • Slight revenue miss vs. consensus — even by $30M — on a stock that was pricing in a beat
  • H1 2026 viewing hours: 97 billion, up only 2% YoY — engagement growth is modest despite subscriber base expansion
  • Stock hit a 52-week low on the print — technical deterioration matters for sentiment-driven assets
